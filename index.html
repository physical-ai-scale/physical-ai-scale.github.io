Workshop Overview

Physical AI systems are increasingly deployed as populations rather than isolated robots: autonomous vehicle fleets, warehouse robots, aerial delivery systems, and collaborative manipulation platforms. This workshop asks when multi-agent structure must shape the design of embodied AI systems, and when single-agent scaling is enough.

We bring together researchers in robot learning, multi-agent systems, autonomous driving, robot foundation models, and population-scale deployment to discuss the theories, algorithms, benchmarks, and system designs needed for multi-agent physical AI.

Theme 1: From single-agent scaling to population behavior

When do generalist robot policies transfer to coordinated multi-agent settings?

Theme 2: Coordination in shared physical spaces

How should agents coordinate when objectives, ownership, and information differ?

Theme 3: Benchmarks, simulation, and deployment

What measurements capture fleet-level safety, throughput, deadlock, and emergence?

Theme 4: Incentives, mechanisms, and shared autonomy

What coordination mechanisms are practical when agents have different owners or objectives?

Call for Papers

We invite short papers up to 4 pages plus references on new, preliminary, or in-progress work in multi-agent and population-scale robot learning. Submissions will be reviewed double-blind; accepted papers will be presented as short talks or posters.
